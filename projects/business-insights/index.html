Training Showcase

A showcase of business insights derived from the NCR dataset, with recommendations hidden behind expandable sections.

1. Revenue Trends

Revenue spikes strongly in November–December (holiday sales), then dips in January. This suggests the business is highly seasonal.

Direction

Highlight this seasonality in dashboards. Recommend inventory ramp-ups & campaign spending increases in Q4, and possibly retention-focused campaigns in January to soften the post-holiday dip.

2. Product Performance

Top Categories: Electronics and Apparel dominate revenue. Subcategories like Mobile Accessories and Women’s Apparel show consistent strength.

Underperformers: Toys and some Beauty products under-index compared to marketing spend.

Direction
  • Reallocate spend toward high-ROI categories.
  • Bundle weaker categories with popular ones (e.g., Toys as holiday add-ons with Electronics).

3. Customer Segments

High-value group: Repeat buyers in the 25–34 age bracket drive the majority of revenue. Low-engagement groups: Older age brackets (55+) and first-time buyers show lower retention.

Direction
  • Create tailored loyalty campaigns for 25–34 repeat buyers.
  • Consider win-back campaigns for older segments.
  • Onboard first-time buyers with post-purchase follow-up sequences.

4. Marketing Channels

Paid Search and Social drive the largest share of new customers. Email performs strongly for repeat purchases. Affiliate and Display lag with weak ROI.

Direction
  • Double down on Paid Search and Social for acquisition.
  • Invest more in Email automation for retention.
  • Audit Affiliate/Display partners to cut waste.

5. Returns

Overall return rate ~7%, but certain subcategories (Women’s Apparel, Electronics Accessories) spike to 12–15%.

Direction
  • Investigate sizing/quality issues in Women’s Apparel.
  • Review Electronics Accessories product descriptions for clarity.
  • Target high-return items for policy or supplier adjustments.

6. Growth Opportunities

Geographic gaps: Revenue skews heavily toward urban areas. Rural penetration remains low. Product gaps: Limited presence in Home & Kitchen compared to competitors.

Direction
  • Explore rural distribution/marketing pilots.
  • Expand catalog in Home & Kitchen to diversify revenue sources.
